Emerging Opportunities: L-1 Visa Expansion

📌 US Commerce Secretary Promotes L-1 Visas:
"The Department of Commerce will have the ability to assist you in getting L-1 visas so that you can bring your employees in to launch the project. You'll train American workers over time, but you can bring your people here and we will set that infrastructure for you." – Howard Lutnick, US Commerce Secretary

The L-1 visa allows multinational companies to transfer employees from overseas offices to US locations. Key features :

  • L-1A: For managers and executives (up to 7 years stay)
  • L-1B: For employees with specialized knowledge (up to 5 years stay)
  • No annual cap – applications accepted year-round
  • Spouses (L-2) can work – work authorization included
  • Path to Green Card – through EB-1 category for managers/executives

Requirements: Employee must have worked for the company for at least one continuous year within the past three years outside the US. The US employer must have a qualifying relationship with the overseas entity (parent, subsidiary, affiliate, or branch) .

EB-3 Green Card – Significant Priority Date Advancement

⚠️ EXCITING EB-3 NEWS (April 2026):

The April 2026 Visa Bulletin shows EB-3 skilled worker priority dates have advanced by roughly two years. This unexpected jump allows many applicants who were previously waiting to now move forward with filing Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status) or consular processing .

However: Given the significant advancement, there is a risk of retrogression. Time is of the essence – act quickly!

The EB-3 "Skilled Workers" category requires at least two years of training or experience. Employers must obtain PERM labor certification and prove no qualified US workers are available. Processing typically takes 1-2 years .

F1/OPT to Green Card – Direct Sponsorship

International students on F-1 visas with OPT work authorization have a clear pathway to permanent residence through employer sponsorship .

  • STEM OPT: 36 months of work authorization
  • EB-3 sponsorship: Direct green card path while working
  • ADEX example: Nurses on F1/OPT can join Green Card sponsorship program

How to Find Visa Sponsorship Jobs

Step 1: Target the Right Companies

Focus on employers with proven sponsorship track records: KPMG, Boeing, American Airlines, ADEX Medical Staffing, Green Doors International, and the hospitality chains listed above .

Step 2: Use Specialized Job Boards

Go beyond general job sites. Use specialized portals like MyVisaJobs and USponsorMe that filter specifically for visa sponsorship positions .

Step 3: Optimize Your Profile for Higher Wages

With the new H-1B weighted selection system, higher-wage positions receive more lottery entries. Focus on skills that command premium salaries .

📋 Required Documents for Visa Sponsorship Application:
  • Valid passport
  • Degree certificates and transcripts (with English translations)
  • Educational credential evaluation (ECA) for foreign degrees
  • Employment verification letters
  • Professional certifications
  • Curriculum vitae (CV) / Resume
  • Passport-style photographs

Step 4: Work with Immigration Counsel

Employers should immediately identify employees and begin working with immigration counsel to evaluate who will need H-1B sponsorship. Tech-enabled firms like Alma offer flat-rate pricing for employment-based visas including H-1B ($3,500), O-1 ($8,000), EB-2 NIW ($10,000), and L-1 ($6,000) .

Final Thoughts – Your Pathway to Working in the US

2026 presents unique opportunities for foreign workers seeking US employment. The EB-3 priority date advancement of approximately two years means many applicants can now move forward with green card processing . The US Commerce Secretary's public endorsement of L-1 visas signals government support for intracompany transfers . And major employers across aviation, healthcare, hospitality, and manufacturing continue to sponsor foreign workers .

Key actions for 2026: Target companies with proven sponsorship records, prepare for the H-1B lottery (next registration March 2027), explore EB-3 green card pathways, and consider L-1 transfers if you work for a multinational company. With the right strategy and employer partner, working legally in the United States is an achievable goal in 2026.
