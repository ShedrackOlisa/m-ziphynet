US Is Hiring Foreign Workers: Visa Sponsorship Jobs Available 2026

The United States continues to face significant labor shortages across multiple sectors in 2026, creating unprecedented opportunities for foreign workers seeking visa sponsorship . With construction, hospitality, technology, healthcare, and manufacturing industries struggling to fill positions, US employers are increasingly turning to international talent through programs like H-1B, H-2B, EB-3, and L-1 visas .

US Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick has also announced federal support for L-1 visas to help companies launch projects and train American workers .

Current US Labor Market & Immigration Landscape (2026)

The US labor market is undergoing a significant transformation in 2026. According to Goldman Sachs, net immigration to the United States has dropped by approximately 80% from historical levels, falling from an average of 1 million annual immigrants to an estimated 200,000 in 2026 . This dramatic reduction in foreign labor supply has created acute labor shortages, particularly in sectors that traditionally rely on immigrant workers .

The construction industry, for example, saw immigrant workers peak at over 25% of the workforce in 2024, but that share is now declining due to federal immigration policies . Certain trades are even more dependent on foreign-born workers:

  • Drywall installers and roofers: Immigrant shares exceed 50% of the workforce
  • Overall tradesworkers: Approximately one-third are immigrants
  • Hospitality and seasonal services: Heavy reliance on H-2B workers
📌 Market Insight: The demand for H-2B workers has surged dramatically. During the January 2026 filing period, the Department of Labor received 10,062 applications requesting more than 162,000 positions—a significant increase from the prior year's 8,759 applications requesting nearly 150,000 positions .

1. H-1B Visa – Specialty Occupations

The H-1B visa remains the primary pathway for tech professionals, engineers, and other specialized workers. For fiscal year 2026, the registration period was March 4-19, 2026, with employers notified by March 31, 2026 .

Key H-1B details for 2026:

  • 65,000 visas under the "Regular Cap" for bachelor's degree holders
  • 20,000 additional visas under the "Master's Cap" for US graduate degree holders
  • $215 registration fee per applicant
  • Beneficiary-centric selection process reducing fraud
  • Petitions for consular processing (applicants abroad) subject to additional $100,000 fee
⚠️ H-1B Update for 2026: USCIS has confirmed that the H-1B cap registration process has been completed for this fiscal year. Additional lottery rounds are unlikely due to the more accurate beneficiary-centric selection process .

2. H-2B Visa – Temporary Non-Agricultural Workers

The H-2B program allows US employers to hire foreign workers for temporary non-agricultural positions . This visa is ideal for hospitality, landscaping, seafood processing, construction, and delivery driver positions.

2026 H-2B highlights:

  • Statutory cap: 66,000 visas per fiscal year (33,000 per half-year)
  • Supplemental visas: 64,716 additional visas authorized for FY 2026
  • The statutory cap for the second half of FY 2026 was reached as of March 10, 2026
  • Supplemental visas are available through September 2026
  • Employers must attest to "irreparable harm" without H-2B workers

Eligibility requirements for H-2B: Employers must demonstrate that there are not enough US workers available, that employing H-2B workers will not adversely affect US worker wages, and that the need is temporary (one-time occurrence, seasonal need, peak load need, or intermittent need) .

3. EB-3 Green Card – Skilled Workers (Permanent Residence)

The EB-3 "Skilled Workers" category is for positions requiring at least two years of training or experience . In exciting news for April 2026, the EB-3 priority date advanced by approximately two years, allowing many applicants who were previously waiting to now move forward with their green card applications .

EB-3 process overview:

  • Employer must complete PERM labor certification
  • Employer must prove no qualified US workers are available
  • Priority date determines place in line (date PERM was filed)
  • Monthly Visa Bulletin shows priority date movement
  • Warning: The significant advancement may retrogress; time is of the essence
🎯 EB-3 Great News (April 2026): The April 2026 Visa Bulletin shows EB-3 skilled worker priority dates have advanced by roughly two years . This unexpected jump means many applicants can now move forward with filing Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status) or proceed with immigrant visa processing at US embassies abroad. Given the risk of retrogression, applicants should act quickly .

4. L-1 Visa – Intracompany Transfers

US Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick has publicly endorsed the L-1 visa program to help companies establish US operations and train American workers . The L-1 visa allows multinational companies to transfer employees from overseas offices to US locations.

L-1 visa subcategories:

  • L-1A: For managers and executives transferred to US offices
  • L-1B: For employees with specialized knowledge of company products/processes

L-1 requirements: Employee must have worked for the company for at least one continuous year within the past three years outside the US. The US employer must have a qualifying relationship with the overseas entity (parent, subsidiary, affiliate, or branch) and be actively operating in the US .

L-1 advantages: No annual cap, spouses may work (L-2 status), and can lead to EB-1 green card pathway for executives/managers .

Step-by-Step Application Process

For H-1B and H-2B Visas:

Step 1: Find an employer willing to sponsor your visa. Look for companies that explicitly mention visa sponsorship or have a history of hiring foreign workers .

Step 2: The employer submits the required petition. For H-1B, this involves registering for the lottery (March). For H-2B, the employer must first obtain a temporary labor certification from DOL .

Step 3: For H-2B, the employer must also file Form I-129 with USCIS . For supplemental H-2B visas, employers must also submit an "irreparable harm" attestation .

Step 4: After USCIS approval, workers outside the US apply for a visa at a US embassy or consulate .

For EB-3 Green Card:

Step 1: Employer obtains PERM labor certification from DOL .

Step 2: Employer files Form I-140 (Immigrant Petition) with USCIS .

Step 3: Wait for priority date to become current (check monthly Visa Bulletin). The April 2026 bulletin saw significant advancement .

Step 4: File Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status) or proceed with consular processing .

📋 Important Documents for EB-3:
  • Valid passport
  • Educational credentials and evaluations
  • Work experience verification (minimum 2 years)
  • PERM labor certification approval
  • Form I-140 approval notice
  • Medical examination results
  • Police clearance certificates

Challenges and Considerations for 2026

While opportunities exist, foreign workers should be aware of the current landscape:

  • Reduced Immigration Levels: Net immigration has dropped approximately 80% from historical levels
  • H-1B Competition: The H-1B lottery remains highly competitive; alternative visas like O-1 should be considered
  • EB-3 Uncertainty: While priority dates have advanced significantly, retrogression is possible; applicants should act quickly
  • Country-Specific Pauses: There is an ongoing temporary pause in immigrant visa issuance for certain countries, including Jamaica
  • H-2B Caps Reached: The statutory H-2B cap for second half FY 2026 was reached on March 10, 2026

Final Thoughts – Is 2026 a Good Time to Seek US Employment?

The paradox of 2026 is that while overall immigration numbers have dropped, employer demand for foreign workers remains extraordinarily high . The construction industry, hospitality sector, and technology field all face acute labor shortages that American workers alone cannot fill .

For qualified foreign workers, multiple pathways exist: H-1B for tech and specialty occupations , H-2B for seasonal and temporary work , EB-3 for skilled permanent positions , and L-1 for intracompany transfers . The dramatic advancement of EB-3 priority dates in April 2026 and the authorization of 64,716 supplemental H-2B visas signal that the US government recognizes the need for foreign labor.

Success requires identifying the right visa pathway, finding an employer willing to sponsor, and preparing a strong application. With the right approach, 2026 can be your year to work legally in the United States.
