📌 What is LMIA? A Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) is a document that a Canadian employer needs to get before hiring a foreign worker. It proves that no Canadian citizen or permanent resident is available to do the job. When you see "LMIA requested" or "LMIA approved" on a job posting, it means the employer is legally authorized to sponsor a foreign worker for a work permit .

Why Delivery Jobs Are in High Demand in Canada

Canada's vast geography and booming consumer demand for fast deliveries have led to a consistent need for skilled drivers. Companies are increasingly looking for international workers to fill delivery roles, often offering visa sponsorship as part of the employment package .

  • E-commerce Growth: Online retail continues to expand, driving demand for last-mile delivery services
  • Supply Chain Needs: Businesses need reliable drivers to move goods between warehouses and customers
  • Driver Shortage: Canada faces a shortage of commercial and delivery drivers, especially in rural and remote areas
  • High Turnover: The transportation sector experiences regular turnover, creating ongoing job openings

No Degree Required – What You Actually Need

Most delivery driver positions in Canada do not require a college degree or university diploma. Official Job Bank postings explicitly state "No degree, certificate or diploma" as the education requirement .

Basic Requirements for Delivery Driver Jobs

  • Valid Driver's License: A valid driver's license is mandatory. Most delivery jobs require a standard Class 5 license (for cars, vans, and light trucks)
  • Clean Driving Record: A clean driving record with no major violations is essential
  • Basic Language Skills: English or French proficiency depending on the province (Quebec positions often require French)
  • Physical Fitness: Many delivery jobs require loading and unloading packages weighing up to 23 kg (50 lbs)
  • Previous Experience: 1 to 7 months to 3-5 years depending on the position
⚠️ IMPORTANT: Never pay for LMIA jobs. Legitimate employers cannot legally recover LMIA-related costs from foreign workers. If anyone asks you to pay fees upfront for a delivery driver position, it is a scam .

Types of Delivery Driver Jobs Available

Delivery driver roles in Canada come in various forms, each with different requirements and pay scales :

  • Parcel/Courier Driver: Deliver packages and documents within cities or regions. Requires Class 5 license .
  • Food Delivery Driver: Deliver restaurant meals to customers. Training provided, flexible hours .
  • Route Driver: Follow established routes for laundry, parts, or product delivery .
  • Long-Haul Truck Driver: Transport goods between provinces. Requires commercial license and more experience.
  • Construction Driver: Deliver materials and equipment to construction sites.

How to Apply for Delivery Driver Jobs with Visa Sponsorship

Step 1: Search for LMIA-Approved Positions

Use Canada's official Job Bank website (jobbank.gc.ca) and filter for positions that mention "LMIA requested" or "LMIA approved." These employers are already in the process of hiring foreign workers .

Step 2: Prepare Your Application

Tailor your resume to highlight your driving experience, clean driving record, and any customer service skills. Mention your willingness to relocate to Canada and your eligibility for a work permit .

Step 3: Submit Your Application

Apply directly through Job Bank or the employer's website. The process is free – do not pay anyone to submit applications for you.

Step 4: Attend Interviews

If shortlisted, you may be invited to a virtual or in-person interview. Be prepared to discuss your driving experience, knowledge of route planning, and customer service abilities.

Step 5: Secure Job Offer and LMIA

Once you receive a job offer, the employer will provide you with the LMIA number and other documentation needed for your work permit application.

Step 6: Apply for Your Work Permit

Submit your work permit application through your IRCC secure account. Processing times average 8-12 weeks.

📋 Documents You'll Need:
  • Valid passport
  • Job offer letter from Canadian employer
  • LMIA number (positive or pending)
  • Valid driver's license (with official translation if not in English/French)
  • Clean driving record abstract
  • Police clearance certificate
  • Medical exam results (if required)

Can Delivery Drivers Get Permanent Residence in Canada?

Yes. After gaining Canadian work experience as a delivery driver, you may be eligible to apply for permanent residency through programs like :

  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC): Requires 12 months of skilled work experience in Canada
  • Provincial Nominee Program (PNP): Many provinces nominate truck and delivery drivers under their streams
  • Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP): For drivers willing to work in Atlantic Canada

Canada's delivery and logistics sector offers a genuine pathway for foreign workers seeking visa sponsorship – without requiring a degree. With LMIA-approved positions currently available, competitive wages ($20-$35/hour), and opportunities for permanent residence, now is an excellent time to pursue delivery driver jobs in Canada .
