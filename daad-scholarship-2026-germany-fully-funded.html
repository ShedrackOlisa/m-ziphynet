DAAD Scholarship 2026 – Fully Funded to Study in Germany for International Students

Advertisement
Published: May 18, 2026 Category: Study in Germany

The DAAD Scholarship 2026 is one of the most prestigious and comprehensive scholarship programmes funded by the German government, offering international students from around the world the opportunity to pursue fully funded master's, MPhil, and PhD degrees at top German universities . The Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst (DAAD), or German Academic Exchange Service, has supported over 2 million scholars since its founding in 1925 .

For the 2026 intake, DAAD is accepting applications from eligible students in developing and emerging countries for development-focused postgraduate programmes, as well as from students worldwide for specialised fields including visual communication, fine art, architecture, design, and music . The scholarship covers full tuition, a monthly stipend, travel allowance, health insurance, and additional family benefits where applicable.

🚀 Key Takeaway: The DAAD Scholarship is a fully funded programme covering full tuition fees, monthly stipends of €934-€1,300 (depending on degree level), round-trip travel allowance, health insurance, rent subsidies, and family allowances. Applications for the 2026 intake have deadlines in late 2025 and early 2026, depending on the programme .

What Does the DAAD Scholarship Cover?

The DAAD Scholarship is a 100% government-funded opportunity that covers virtually all expenses associated with studying in Germany . The comprehensive benefits package includes:

BenefitAmount/Details
Monthly Stipend €934 for master's students / €992-€1,300 for PhD candidates (€1,400 starting February 2026)
Full Tuition Coverage Complete waiver of all tuition and participation fees
Travel Allowance Round-trip airfare between home country and Germany
Health Insurance Medical, accident, and personal liability insurance coverage
Study/Research Subsidy Funding to support academic research activities and materials
Rent Subsidy Monthly assistance with accommodation costs (if applicable)
Family Allowance Financial support for spouses and/or children (if applicable)
German Language Courses 2, 4, or 6 months of optional language training before studies begin
📌 Important Note: Germany is renowned for offering tuition-free education at public universities for both domestic and international students . The DAAD scholarship primarily covers living expenses, travel, and insurance, making it even more valuable as tuition is already minimal or non-existent at most public institutions.

Types of DAAD Scholarships Available

DAAD offers multiple scholarship programmes catering to different academic levels and backgrounds :

1. DAAD International Postgraduate Scholarship

This programme supports students pursuing master's degrees, MPhil, or short courses at German universities. Fields of study include visual communication and film, fine art, architecture, design, and music . The scholarship duration varies between 12 and 24 months depending on the chosen course and university .

2. DAAD EPOS (Development-Related Postgraduate Courses)

EPOS scholarships are specifically designed for students from developing and emerging countries . These scholarships require at least two years of professional work experience and focus on development-oriented fields. Benefits include:

  • €992 monthly stipend for master's students
  • €1,300-€1,400 monthly stipend for doctoral candidates
  • Full tuition coverage
  • Travel allowance and health insurance
  • Accommodation and family allowance in certain cases

3. In-Country/In-Region Scholarship Programme

This programme focuses on building local capacity in Sub-Saharan Africa by supporting postgraduate education at selected universities across Kenya, Ghana, Nigeria, Rwanda, Tanzania, Uganda, South Africa, Malawi, and Burkina Faso . Benefits include monthly stipends covering living expenses, full tuition fees, and annual research allowances .

4. Helmut-Schmidt Programme (Master's Scholarships)

This programme is open to candidates committed to contributing to the social and economic development of their home country. It offers a monthly scholarship of €992 for selected master's programmes at German higher education institutions in fields such as social security, development and governance, public policy, peace and conflict studies, and public management .

5. Bi-nationally Supervised Doctoral Degrees (Cotutelle)

For doctoral students undertaking joint supervision between a German university and their home university. The scholarship should start with the winter semester (October 2026), with a deadline of 1 December 2025 .

Eligibility Requirements

Eligibility criteria vary by programme, but general requirements include :

  • Nationality: Open to students from all countries (some programmes target developing/emerging countries)
  • Bachelor's Degree: Must hold a first academic degree (minimum 4-year bachelor's or equivalent)
  • Academic Excellence: Strong academic record with high grades
  • Work Experience: EPOS programmes require at least 2 years of relevant professional experience
  • Recent Graduation: As a rule, applicants should not have graduated more than 6 years before the application deadline
  • Language Proficiency: English (IELTS/TOEFL) or German (DSH/TestDaF) depending on the programme
  • German Residency Restriction: Applicants may not have been resident in Germany for more than 15 months at the application deadline
⚠️ IMPORTANT RESTRICTIONS:
  • If you have already started your doctorate, the start of your doctorate should not be longer than 3 years ago
  • For applicants from human medicine, veterinary medicine, and dentistry, special regulations apply
  • The scholarship typically starts with the winter semester (October); a summer semester start is not possible

Fields of Study Covered by DAAD

DAAD scholarships support a wide range of academic disciplines :

  • Visual Communication and Film: Digital media, filmmaking, and design
  • Fine Art: Painting, sculpture, and other visual arts
  • Architecture: Urban planning, building design, and construction management
  • Design: Product design, fashion design, and industrial design
  • Music: Classical, contemporary, composition, and performance studies
  • Engineering, Natural Sciences, Agricultural Sciences, Social Sciences, and Public/Environmental Health
  • Development and Governance, Public Policy, Peace and Conflict Studies

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Find Your Programme

Browse the official DAAD scholarship database at www.funding-guide.de. Filter by subject, country of origin, and academic level to find programmes matching your profile .

Step 2: Review Programme Requirements

Each DAAD scholarship has specific eligibility criteria, required documents, and application deadlines. Visit the programme's official website for detailed information .

Step 3: Prepare Required Documents

Gather the following documents before starting your application :

  • DAAD Application Form – Completed and signed
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) – Tabular format, max 3 pages (Europass format preferred)
  • Motivation Letter – 1-3 pages explaining your academic and professional reasons for choosing Germany and the specific programme
  • Research Proposal – For PhD applicants, max 10 pages describing your research project
  • Academic Transcripts and Degree Certificates – Certified copies with English/German translations
  • Letters of Recommendation – 2-3 letters from university professors or employers
  • Proof of Language Proficiency – IELTS/TOEFL for English or DSH/TestDaF for German
  • Copy of Passport – Valid passport identification page
  • Portfolio – Required for applicants in Art, Design, and Music fields
  • Proof of Work Experience – For EPOS programmes, minimum 2 years
📝 Pro Tip for PhD Applicants:
  • Include a confirmation from your German academic supervisor (host institution acceptance)
  • Provide a detailed schedule for planned research work for the entire funding period
  • Include a declaration on safety-relevant and ethical aspects of your research project

Step 4: Submit Your Application

Applications are submitted through the DAAD Portal (meinDAAD) . Register for an account, complete the online application, and upload all required documents in the 'Attachments' section.

📌 Application Notes:
  • Certificates and translations may be scanned in non-certified form for initial submission
  • DAAD reserves the right to request certified copies of documents later
  • If submitting documents in your national language, include German or English translations
  • The DAAD portal only appears during the current application period

Step 5: Await Selection Results

Applications undergo a rigorous evaluation process:

  • Administrative Check: Verifying eligibility and document completeness
  • Selection Committee Review: Evaluation by academic experts (typically April/May)
  • Interview (if shortlisted): Some programmes invite top candidates for interviews
  • Final Notification: Successful candidates are notified, with funding typically starting in October

DAAD Scholarship for US and Canadian Citizens

Highly qualified final-year undergraduate students or recent graduates from US or Canadian universities may apply for the DAAD Study Scholarship for a full master's degree programme in Germany . Key details include:

  • Eligibility: US or Canadian citizens or permanent residents
  • Degree Completion: Must have completed last degree no longer than 6 years ago
  • German Residency: May not have been living in Germany for more than 15 consecutive months by application deadline
  • Funding Period: 10-24 months depending on the study programme
  • Application Deadline: 31 October 2026 (for 2027 start)

Post-Scholarship Opportunities

DAAD scholars are expected to return to their home countries after completing their studies and apply the knowledge and skills gained to contribute to local development . Many DAAD alumni go on to become leaders in government, academia, business, and civil society in their home countries.

For those seeking to stay in Germany post-graduation, international students with a German degree may apply for an 18-month residence permit for job-seeking, and eventually qualify for permanent residence .

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I apply for multiple DAAD scholarships?

Yes, you may apply for multiple programmes, but you can only accept one scholarship if selected.

Is there an age limit for DAAD?

There is no strict age limit, but applicants typically should have graduated within the last 6 years .

Do I need to know German to apply?

Not necessarily. Many DAAD programmes are taught entirely in English. However, German language skills are beneficial and some programmes offer German language courses as part of the scholarship .

Can I work while on the DAAD scholarship?

DAAD scholarships are designed to cover living expenses, allowing scholars to focus on their studies. Part-time work regulations vary by German state and visa type.

What is the acceptance rate for DAAD?

DAAD is highly competitive, with acceptance rates varying by programme (typically 10-20%). Strong academic records, clear research proposals, and development relevance are essential.

Final Thoughts – Is DAAD Right for You?

The DAAD Scholarship is one of the world's most prestigious and comprehensive international scholarship programmes, offering fully funded opportunities to study in Germany at leading universities . With generous monthly stipends (€934-€1,300+), full tuition coverage, travel allowance, health insurance, and family benefits, DAAD removes financial barriers to German education .

Germany's public universities already offer minimal or zero tuition fees ; the DAAD scholarship makes studying in Germany even more accessible by covering living expenses. Whether you're interested in engineering, arts, social sciences, or development studies, DAAD offers programmes tailored to your academic and professional goals.

While most deadlines for the 2026 intake have passed, you can start preparing now for the 2027 intake. Use the coming months to research programmes, contact potential supervisors, prepare your documents, and strengthen your language skills.
