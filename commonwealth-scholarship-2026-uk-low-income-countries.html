Commonwealth Scholarship 2026 – Study in the UK for Students from Low-Income Countries

Published: May 18, 2026 11 min read Category: Study in UK

The Commonwealth Scholarship 2026 is one of the most prestigious fully funded scholarship programmes funded by the UK Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office (FCDO), offering talented students from low and middle-income Commonwealth countries the opportunity to pursue a master's degree at leading UK universities . The scholarship is designed for individuals who could not otherwise afford to study in the UK and who have the potential to contribute to sustainable development in their home countries .

Since its establishment, the Commonwealth Scholarship Commission (CSC) has supported over 30,000 scholars from Commonwealth countries worldwide . For the 2026-2027 academic year, the CSC offers multiple types of scholarships including Commonwealth Master's Scholarships, Commonwealth Shared Scholarships, and Commonwealth Distance Learning Scholarships .

🚀 Key Takeaway: The Commonwealth Scholarship is a fully funded UK government scholarship for students from eligible low and middle-income Commonwealth countries. Benefits include full tuition fees, a monthly living stipend (£1,452-£1,781 depending on location), return economy airfare, thesis allowance, study travel grant, and other allowances . Applications for the 2026 intake closed in October-December 2025, but use this guide to prepare for the 2027 cycle .

Types of Commonwealth Scholarships for 2026

The Commonwealth Scholarship Commission offers several scholarship schemes for the 2026-2027 academic year .

1. Commonwealth Master's Scholarships

These scholarships are for candidates from eligible low and middle-income Commonwealth countries to undertake full-time taught master's study at a UK university . Funded by the UK FCDO, these scholarships enable talented individuals to gain knowledge and skills required for sustainable development [citation:1][citation:9].

2. Commonwealth Shared Scholarships

The Shared Scholarship Scheme is a joint initiative between the CSC and UK universities to support scholarships for students from developing Commonwealth countries . For 2026 entry, applications opened on 12 November 2025 and closed on 9 December 2025 [citation:3][citation:4][citation:5]. The University of Exeter offered three Shared Scholarships for 2026 entry, covering full tuition fees, living costs, airfares, and allowances [citation:4].

3. Commonwealth Distance Learning Scholarships

These scholarships are for candidates from low and middle-income Commonwealth countries applying for part-time master's study by distance learning . The 2026 application cycle opened on 17 February 2026 and closed on 31 March 2026 . These scholarships cover full tuition fees and allow students to remain in their home country while studying [citation:2][citation:6].

What Does the Commonwealth Scholarship Cover?

The Commonwealth Master's Scholarship is a comprehensive funding package that covers virtually all expenses associated with studying a one-year master's degree in the UK [citation:10]. The scholarship includes:

BenefitDetails
Full Tuition Fees Complete coverage of tuition fees at the overseas rate for the duration of the course [citation:3][citation:5]
Monthly Living Stipend £1,452 per month (for scholars studying outside London) or £1,781 per month (for scholars studying inside London) [citation:10]
Return Airfare Approved round-trip economy airfare from your home country to the UK [citation:4][citation:10]
Warm Clothing Allowance One-off allowance for scholars studying in the UK [citation:10]

Study Travel Grant Contribution toward study-related travel costs [citation:8]
Arrival Allowance One-off payment to help with initial settling-in costs [citation:8]
Family Allowances For single, divorced, or widowed parents with children under 16 [citation:10]
Disability Support Allowance Additional allowance for scholars with disabilities [citation:10]

Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for a Commonwealth Master's Scholarship, applicants must meet the following criteria [citation:1][citation:5][citation:7]:

Citizenship and Residency

  • Be a citizen of or have been granted refugee status by an eligible Commonwealth country, or be a British Protected Person [citation:1][citation:5]
  • Be permanently resident in an eligible Commonwealth country [citation:1][citation:2]
  • Not have studied or worked for one (academic) year or more in a high-income country [citation:5]

Academic Requirements

  • Hold a first degree of at least upper second-class (2:1) honours standard [citation:1][citation:3][citation:7]
  • Alternatively, a lower second-class degree (2:2) and a relevant postgraduate qualification (usually a master's degree) may be considered [citation:1][citation:5]
  • The CSC would not normally fund a second master's degree. If applying for a second master's degree, you must provide justification [citation:1][citation:7]
  • Be available to start academic studies in the UK by September 2026 [citation:1][citation:5]

Financial Need

  • Be unable to afford to study in the UK without this scholarship [citation:1][citation:2][citation:5]

Eligible Commonwealth Countries

Commonwealth Master's Scholarships are open to candidates from the following low and middle-income Commonwealth countries [citation:3][citation:9]:

  • Africa: Botswana, Cameroon, Eswatini, Gabon, The Gambia, Ghana, Kenya, Lesotho, Malawi, Mauritius, Mozambique, Namibia, Nigeria, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, South Africa, Tanzania, Togo, Uganda, Zambia
  • Asia: Bangladesh, India, Malaysia, Maldives, Pakistan, Sri Lanka
  • Caribbean & Americas: Belize, Dominica, Grenada, Guyana, Saint Lucia, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines
  • Pacific: Fiji, Kiribati, Nauru, Papua New Guinea, Samoa, Solomon Islands, Tonga, Tuvalu, Vanuatu
📌 Development Themes: Commonwealth Scholarships are offered under six development themes [citation:6][citation:9]:
  • Science and technology for development
  • Strengthening health systems and capacity
  • Promoting innovation and entrepreneurship / global prosperity
  • Strengthening peace, security and governance
  • Strengthening resilience and response to crises
  • Access, inclusion and opportunity

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Check Your Eligibility

Confirm that your country is on the eligible list and that you meet all citizenship, residency, and academic requirements .

Step 2: Find a Nominating Agency

Important: The Commonwealth Scholarship Commission (CSC) does not accept direct applications from individuals . Candidates must apply through a National Nominating Agency or an authorized non-governmental/charitable organization in their home country [citation:9][citation:10]. These nominating agencies include:

  • Government ministries (e.g., Ministry of Education)
  • Presidential or Prime Minister's offices
  • National development agencies
  • Authorized NGOs and charitable organizations

Contact your nominating agency first for their specific advice, rules for applying, and their own closing date for applications [citation:9].

Step 3: Research UK Universities and Courses

Identify eligible master's programmes at UK universities. Check that your chosen course aligns with one of the CSC's development themes . Different scholarship types have different eligible programmes:

  • Commonwealth Shared Scholarships: Only available for specific courses at partner universities [citation:3][citation:5]
  • Commonwealth Master's Scholarships: Available for any full-time taught master's (MBA programmes are not eligible) [citation:9]

Step 4: Apply for University Admission

Apply for your chosen master's programme well in advance of the CSC scholarship deadline so you can receive an admission decision. You must have an unconditional offer or be able to meet the conditions by the final shortlisting stage [citation:5][citation:9].

Step 5: Prepare Your Application Documents

Gather the following documents for your CSC application:

  • Application form (completed via CSC's online application system, "CSC Central") [citation:2]
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV)
  • Academic transcripts and degree certificates (certified copies)
  • Proof of English language proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent)
  • Two academic or professional reference letters
  • Study plan or research proposal
  • Supporting statement from your nominating agency
  • Copy of passport
📝 Key Application Tips:
  • Start early: The application process requires coordination with your nominating agency, which has its own internal deadlines before the CSC deadline [citation:9]
  • Secure university admission first: You generally need an offer from a UK university before the CSC will consider your scholarship application
  • Write a compelling study plan: The CSC evaluates applications based on candidate merit, quality of study plan, and potential impact on the development of your home country [citation:2]
  • Demonstrate financial need: You must show you cannot afford to study in the UK without this scholarship [citation:1][citation:3]

Step 6: Submit Your Application via CSC Central

All applications must be made using the CSC's online application system, CSC Central . You can access the system via the CSC website when applications are open. The CSC cannot accept any applications or documentation not submitted via the online application system [citation:2][citation:7].

Step 7: Await Selection Results

Applications undergo a rigorous evaluation process:

  • Initial screening: By your nominating agency
  • Academic review: By the CSC based on academic merit, study plan quality, and development impact [citation:2]
  • Final selection: Approved by the Commonwealth Scholarship Commission
  • Notification: Successful candidates notified (typically March-May)

Post-Scholarship Requirements

Commonwealth Scholars are required to return to their home country after completing their studies in the UK [citation:3]. The CSC will only consider requests to extend leave to remain in the UK post-award for those wishing to move from master's to PhD study, with strict conditions applying [citation:3].

Upon returning home, scholars are expected to apply the knowledge and skills gained during their UK education to contribute to their home country's sustainable development .

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I apply directly to the Commonwealth Scholarship Commission?

No. The CSC does not accept direct applications. You must apply through a national nominating agency in your home country [citation:9][citation:10].

What is the age limit for Commonwealth Scholarships?

There is no strict age limit. However, preference is generally given to mid-career professionals and recent graduates who can demonstrate the potential to contribute to development in their home country [citation:10].

Do I need an IELTS or TOEFL score?

Yes, you must demonstrate proficiency in English to satisfy your UK university's admission requirements. The CSC requires that universities certify that candidates are sufficiently proficient in English [citation:3].

Can I apply if I have already studied in the UK?

Generally, you must not have undertaken studies lasting one year or more in a developed country [citation:3][citation:5].

Can I bring my family with me?

The CSC may provide family allowances for single, divorced, or widowed parents with children under 16 [citation:10]. Additional allowances may be available for scholars with disabilities [citation:10].

What is the acceptance rate for Commonwealth Scholarships?

Commonwealth Scholarships are highly competitive, with thousands of applications received annually for a limited number of awards (typically 200-300 master's scholarships per year). Strong academic records, compelling study plans, and demonstrated development impact are essential for success.

Final Thoughts – Is Commonwealth Right for You?

The Commonwealth Scholarship is one of the world's most prestigious and comprehensive international scholarship programmes, offering fully funded opportunities to study at leading UK universities for students from low and middle-income Commonwealth countries [citation:10]. With full tuition coverage, generous monthly stipends (£1,452-£1,781), return airfare, and additional allowances, Commonwealth removes financial barriers to UK education .

However, Commonwealth is about more than funding – it is a leadership development programme that invests in future change-makers who will return to their home countries and contribute to sustainable development . If you have strong academic credentials (upper second-class minimum), demonstrable financial need, and a clear vision for how a UK master's degree will help you drive development in your home country, the Commonwealth Scholarship could be your pathway to an extraordinary academic and professional journey.
