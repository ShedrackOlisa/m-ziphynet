Chevening Scholarship 2026/2027 – Study in the UK Fully Funded

Advertisement
Published: May 18, 2026 Category: Study in UK

The Chevening Scholarship 2026/2027 is the UK Government's flagship international scholarship programme, offering fully funded master's degrees to outstanding professionals from over 160 countries and territories . Established in 1983, Chevening is funded by the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) and partner organisations, and has supported more than 55,000 alumni worldwide .

For the 2027/28 academic year, applications will open in August 2026, with scholars beginning their studies in September/October 2027 . This prestigious scholarship covers full tuition fees, a monthly living stipend, return airfare to the UK, visa costs, and access to exclusive networking events and leadership development programmes .

🚀 Key Takeaway: Chevening is a fully funded, one-year master's scholarship sponsored by the UK government. Applications for the 2027/28 cycle open in August 2026 . Benefits include full tuition, monthly living stipend (£1,236-£1,624 depending on location), return airfare, visa fees, and access to a global network of over 55,000 alumni .

What Does the Chevening Scholarship Cover?

Chevening offers a comprehensive funding package that covers all major expenses associated with studying a one-year master's degree in the UK . The scholarship includes:

  • Full Tuition Fees: Complete coverage of university tuition fees for any eligible one-year master's programme
  • Monthly Living Stipend (Personal Living Allowance):
    • £1,236 per month for scholars studying outside London
    • £1,624 per month for scholars studying inside London
  • Return Economy Airfare: Round-trip flights to and from your home country
  • Visa Application Costs: Coverage of UK student visa fees
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS): Cost of UK healthcare surcharge included
  • Arrival Allowance: One-time payment to help with initial settling-in costs
  • Departure Allowance: Assistance with end-of-programme expenses
  • Travel Top-Up Allowance: Additional travel support within the UK
  • TB Testing Contribution: Up to £75 for required tuberculosis testing
📌 Note on MBA Funding: Chevening does fund MBA programmes, but with a tuition cap of £22,000 . If your MBA tuition exceeds this amount, you will need to cover the difference through other means.

Eligibility Requirements for Chevening 2026/2027

Chevening Scholarships are open to citizens of Chevening-eligible countries . Below are the core eligibility requirements:

Basic Eligibility

  • Citizenship: Must be a citizen of a Chevening-eligible country (over 160 countries and territories)
  • Return Requirement: Must return to your home country for a minimum of two years after completing your scholarship
  • Undergraduate Degree: Must hold an undergraduate degree that will enable entry into a UK postgraduate programme (typically equivalent to a UK upper second-class 2:1 honours degree)
  • Work Experience: Minimum two years of work experience (equivalent to 2,800 hours), which can include full-time, part-time, volunteer, or paid/unpaid internships

Work Experience Explained

The work experience requirement can be satisfied through various forms of professional and voluntary activities :

  • Full-time employment
  • Part-time employment
  • Volunteer work
  • Paid or unpaid internships
  • Work completed before or after graduation from your undergraduate degree

Ineligible Applicants

The following individuals are not eligible to apply :

  • British citizens or dual nationals where one nationality is British
  • Employees (or former employees within two years) of His Majesty's Government, the British Council, or Chevening partner organisations
  • Relatives of employees of the above organisations
  • Individuals who have previously received a UK government-funded scholarship
  • Applicants who have already studied in the UK with a Chevening or other UK government-funded award

Eligible Courses – What Can You Study?

Chevening Scholarships fund full-time, taught master's degrees (not research-focused MRes programmes) at any UK university . The following programmes are eligible:

  • Master of Arts (MA)
  • Master of Science (MSc)
  • Master of Laws (LLM)
  • Master of Business Administration (MBA) – subject to £22,000 tuition cap
  • Other taught master's programmes
⚠️ EXCLUDED COURSES: Chevening does NOT fund :
  • Distance learning programmes
  • Part-time master's degrees
  • Research-focused MRes programmes
  • Doctoral (PhD) programmes
  • Pre-sessional or foundation courses
  • Courses shorter than 9 months or longer than 12 months

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Check Your Country's Eligibility

Visit the Chevening website and navigate to your country's specific page. Some countries have priority subject areas or additional eligibility criteria .

Step 2: Research UK Universities and Courses

Unlike Fulbright, Chevening does NOT pre-select universities for you. You are responsible for researching and applying to UK universities independently . You must:

  • Identify three eligible UK master's programmes
  • Meet each university's entry requirements
  • Secure an unconditional offer from at least one programme by the July deadline
  • List these three courses in your Chevening application

Step 3: Prepare Your Application Documents

You will need the following for your Chevening application :

  • Two References/Recommendation Letters: Academic or professional references. You must contact your referees directly to obtain their letters. Chevening does not contact referees on your behalf
  • Academic Transcripts and Degree Certificates: Undergraduate qualifications
  • English Language Proficiency: IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent (if required by your chosen universities)
  • University Admission Proof: Not required at the time of Chevening application, but must be secured by July deadline

Step 4: Complete the Online Application

Submit your application through the official Chevening website at https://www.chevening.org/apply/ . The application includes :

  • Personal information and eligibility verification
  • Education and work experience history (up to 15 entries)
  • University course selections (three choices)
  • Two reference/referee details
  • Four essay questions (detailed below)
📝 Chevening Application Essays (Four Questions):
  1. Leadership and Influence: Demonstrate your leadership qualities and ability to influence others
  2. Networking Skills: Show how you build and maintain professional relationships
  3. Studying in the UK: Explain why you want to study in the UK and how your chosen courses align with your career goals
  4. Career Plan: Outline your post-graduation career plans and how Chevening will help you achieve them

Step 5: Submit and Wait for Sifting

After submission, your application undergoes an initial eligibility check. Qualifying applications are then sifted by independent readers who score your essays and overall suitability .

Step 6: Attend the Interview

Shortlisted candidates are invited to a panel interview in their home country (typically February-April) . The interview is conducted in English and assesses your leadership potential, networking abilities, and fit for the Chevening programme. Preparation is essential.

Step 7: Receive University Offers

If you pass the interview, you receive a conditional Chevening offer. You must then secure an unconditional offer from at least one of the three UK universities you listed in your application by the July deadline (typically 9 July) .

Step 8: Final Chevening Award

Once you provide proof of an unconditional university offer and meet all conditions, you receive your final Chevening award and can begin the visa application process .

Selection Criteria – What is Chevening Looking For?

The Chevening selection process evaluates candidates based on four core competencies :

  • Leadership Potential: Demonstrated ability to lead, inspire, and influence others in professional, academic, or community settings
  • Networking Abilities: Capacity to build and maintain professional relationships across cultures and sectors
  • Academic Excellence: Strong academic record with a degree equivalent to UK 2:1 honours or higher
  • Clear Vision for the Future: Well-defined career goals and a realistic plan for how the UK master's degree will help achieve them
💡 Tips for a Strong Application:
  • Use specific examples from your work, volunteer, or leadership experiences
  • Demonstrate how you have already made an impact in your community or workplace
  • Connect your UK master's degree to specific career goals in your home country
  • Show awareness of Chevening's values: openness, integrity, and innovation
  • Research your chosen UK universities thoroughly to explain why they are the best fit

Post-Scholarship Requirements – The Return Period

Chevening Scholars are required to return to their home country for a minimum of two years after completing their studies in the UK . This "return period" is a fundamental condition of the scholarship and cannot be waived.

During this period, scholars are expected to apply the skills and knowledge gained during their UK education to contribute to their home country's development. Many Chevening alumni go on to become leaders in government, business, civil society, and academia.

Chevening Alumni Network – A Global Community

One of the most valuable benefits of a Chevening Scholarship is access to the global Chevening alumni network, which includes over 55,000 members worldwide . As a Chevening Scholar, you will :

  • Connect with leaders and experts across government, business, and civil society
  • Participate in a year-long programme of events, workshops, and talks designed to build networks and develop leadership skills
  • Receive invitations to exclusive Chevening conferences and networking events
  • Join country-specific alumni chapters that organise professional development and social activities

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

When does the Chevening application open for 2027/28?

Applications for the 2027/28 academic year will open in August 2026 . The deadline is typically in October 2026.

Can I apply if I don't have a university offer yet?

Yes. You can apply for Chevening without having secured a university offer. However, you must obtain an unconditional offer from at least one of your three chosen universities by the July deadline (approximately 9 July) to receive the final scholarship award .

Do I need IELTS or TOEFL to apply?

Chevening does not require English test scores at the application stage. However, your chosen UK universities will require proof of English proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent) to issue an unconditional offer. You should plan to take an English test early enough to meet the July university offer deadline.

Can I apply if I have previously studied in the UK?

Generally, applicants who have previously studied in the UK with a UK government-funded scholarship are not eligible . Self-funded study or study funded by other sources may be acceptable – check your country's specific guidelines.

What is the age limit for Chevening?

There is no upper age limit for Chevening Scholarships. The programme focuses on professional experience and leadership potential rather than age.

Can I apply with a spouse or dependents?

Yes, Chevening Scholars may bring dependents to the UK. However, the scholarship does not provide additional funding for dependents. You must demonstrate sufficient additional funds to support any accompanying family members.

How competitive is Chevening?

Chevening is highly competitive, with thousands of applications received annually for approximately 1,500-1,800 awards worldwide. Strong leadership experience, clear career goals, and well-written essays are essential for success.

Final Thoughts – Is Chevening Right for You?

The Chevening Scholarship is one of the world's most prestigious international scholarships, offering a fully funded opportunity to study a master's degree at any UK university . With comprehensive coverage including full tuition, living stipend (£1,236-£1,624/month), return airfare, and visa costs, Chevening removes financial barriers to UK education .

However, Chevening is about more than funding – it is a leadership development programme that invests in future global leaders who will return to their home countries and make a difference . If you have strong leadership potential, at least two years of work experience, and a clear vision for how a UK master's degree will help you achieve your career goals, Chevening could be your pathway to an extraordinary academic and professional journey.
