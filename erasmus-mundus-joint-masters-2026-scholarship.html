Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters 2026 – Fully Funded Scholarship to Study in Europe

Advertisement
Published: May 18, 2026 Category: Study in Europe

The Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters (EMJM) 2026 is a prestigious, fully funded scholarship programme funded by the European Union, offering international students the opportunity to earn a master's degree while studying in multiple European countries . For the 2026-2028 academic cycle, applications are open across dozens of specialized programmes ranging from engineering and data science to journalism and security studies .

Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters are high-level integrated international study programmes delivered by a consortium of at least three higher education institutions from at least three different European countries . Students benefit from a unique cross-cultural academic experience, graduating with either a joint degree or multiple degrees from the participating universities.

🚀 Key Takeaway: The Erasmus Mundus scholarship is a fully funded, two-year master's programme that covers full tuition fees, provides a €1,400 monthly living stipend for up to 24 months, includes travel allowances and health insurance, and allows students to study in 2-4 different European countries . Applications for the 2026 intake are now open with deadlines varying by programme (typically October 2025 – June 2026).

What Does the Erasmus Mundus Scholarship Cover?

The Erasmus Mundus scholarship is a 100% EU-funded opportunity that covers virtually all expenses associated with studying a two-year master's degree in Europe . The comprehensive benefits package includes :

  • Full Tuition Fee Waiver: Complete coverage of all tuition and participation fees for the entire two-year programme
  • Monthly Living Stipend: €1,400 per month for up to 24 months (totaling €33,600 over two years) to cover housing, food, and daily expenses
  • Travel Allowance: Contribution toward round-trip airfare and relocation expenses
  • Health and Liability Insurance: Comprehensive medical coverage throughout the study period
  • Visa and Installation Costs: Support for visa application fees and settling-in expenses
📌 Additional Benefits: Some programmes also offer Erasmus+ stipends (up to €6,000) for students not awarded the full scholarship, providing financial support for the second year of study .

This multidisciplinary programme covers the interactions between radiation and microelectronics/photonics, two key enabling technologies for Europe's future. Offered jointly by six universities: University of Ghent (Belgium), University Jean Monnet (France), University of Jyväskylä (Finland), Katholieke Universiteit Leuven (Belgium), University of Montpellier (France), and Vrije University Brussels (Belgium) .

Eligibility Requirements

Eligibility requirements vary slightly by programme, but the following general criteria apply to most Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters scholarships :

Basic Eligibility

  • Nationality: Open to students from all countries worldwide
  • Bachelor's Degree: Must hold a Bachelor's degree (minimum 180 ECTS credits or equivalent) from a recognized institution
  • Academic Excellence: Strong academic record; some programmes require being in the top 10% of your class
  • English Proficiency: Minimum B2 level according to CEFR (typically IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL iBT 85-92)
  • Degree Completion: Students in their final year of bachelor's may apply conditionally, but must graduate before the programme starts (typically by mid-July)

English Language Requirements

Most programmes require proof of English proficiency through one of the following recognized tests :

  • IELTS Academic: Minimum overall score of 6.5
  • TOEFL iBT: Minimum score of 85-92 (depending on programme)
  • Pearson Test of English (PTE) Academic: Minimum score of 62-70
  • Cambridge C1 Advanced or C2 Proficiency: Grade A, B, or C
📌 English Exemptions: You may be exempt from English testing if:
  • You completed a degree programme taught entirely in English
  • You are a native English speaker from an English-speaking country
  • You completed upper secondary education in English in an EU/EEA country, UK, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, or other recognized countries
Note: Test results must typically be valid until the end of the application period and no more than two years old .

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Find Your Programme

Browse the official Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters catalogue at the European Commission's Erasmus+ website. You can search by academic field, country, or keyword .

Step 2: Review Programme Requirements

Each EMJM programme has its own specific eligibility criteria, required documents, and application deadlines. Visit the programme's official website for detailed information .

Step 3: Prepare Your Application Documents

Gather the following documents before starting your application:

  • Academic Transcripts: Official transcripts from all post-secondary institutions attended (with certified English translations if not originally in English)
  • Degree Certificates/Diplomas: Proof of bachelor's degree completion (or proof of current enrollment if still a student)
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV): Detailed CV highlighting academic and professional experience
  • Statement of Purpose/Motivation Letter: Explaining why you want to join the programme and how it aligns with your career goals
  • Letters of Recommendation: Typically 2-3 academic or professional references
  • English Proficiency Test Scores: IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, or Cambridge certificate (if applicable)
  • Copy of Passport: Valid passport identification page

Step 4: Submit Your Online Application

Applications are submitted directly to the consortium coordinating the programme, typically through the programme's dedicated application portal . Complete all sections carefully, as incomplete applications will not be considered.

📝 Pro Tip: Start early! The Erasmus Mundus application requires multiple documents, letters of recommendation, and a compelling statement of purpose. Give yourself at least 2-3 months to prepare a competitive application.

Step 5: Await Selection Results

After submission, applications go through a rigorous evaluation process:

  • Administrative Eligibility Check: Verifying that all required documents are submitted and eligibility criteria are met
  • Academic Evaluation: Assessment of academic qualifications and relevance to the programme
  • Interview (if shortlisted): Many programmes invite top candidates for online interviews
  • Final Selection: Admission letters sent to successful candidates

Step 6: Accept Offer and Apply for Visa

If selected, you'll receive a formal offer letter. Accept the offer and begin the student visa application process for your host country/countries. The scholarship includes support for visa costs .

Selection Criteria – What Are They Looking For?

Erasmus Mundus scholarships are highly competitive. Selection committees evaluate candidates based on :

  • Academic Excellence: Strong academic record; top-ranking students (top 10% of class) are preferred
  • Relevant Background: Degree and coursework aligned with the programme's focus areas
  • Motivation and Fit: Compelling statement of purpose demonstrating genuine interest in the programme and cross-cultural experience
  • Recommendation Letters: Strong endorsements from professors or employers
  • Diversity: Erasmus Mundus values geographical and cultural diversity; students from all countries are encouraged to apply

The Unique Erasmus Mundus Experience – Studying in Multiple Countries

Unlike traditional master's programmes, Erasmus Mundus students study in at least two different European countries during the two-year programme . This unique structure offers:

  • Cross-Cultural Exposure: Experience different education systems, languages, and cultures
  • Multi-University Degree: Graduate with either a joint degree or multiple degrees from partner institutions
  • European Network: Build professional connections across the continent
  • Language Learning: Opportunity to learn new languages while studying

Post-Graduation Opportunities – Work Visas and Careers

Graduates of Erasmus Mundus programmes are well-positioned for international careers. Many participating countries offer post-study work visa options. For example, Mundus Journalism graduates are eligible for a work visa in both Denmark and their second-year host country, offering flexibility to gain professional experience in Europe after graduation .

Beyond financial assistance, Erasmus Mundus programmes provide career guidance resources including one-on-one counselling, workshops, and networking events to help students build employability skills and prepare for the transition from student to professional life .

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I apply for multiple Erasmus Mundus programmes?

Yes, you may apply to multiple EMJM programmes simultaneously. However, you can only accept one scholarship offer if selected for multiple programmes.

Is there an age limit for Erasmus Mundus?

No, there is no age limit. The scholarship is open to students of all ages who meet the academic requirements.

Do I need to know a European language besides English?

Not for admission. Most programmes are taught entirely in English. However, learning the local language of your host countries is encouraged and can enhance your experience.

Can I work while on the Erasmus Mundus scholarship?

Regulations vary by host country. Generally, international students in Europe may work part-time (typically up to 20 hours per week) during their studies. Check the specific rules for your host countries.

What is the acceptance rate for Erasmus Mundus?

Erasmus Mundus is highly competitive, with acceptance rates typically ranging from 5-15% depending on the programme.

Can I apply if I already have a master's degree?

In most cases, yes. However, preference may be given to applicants who have not previously received an Erasmus Mundus scholarship or a similar EU-funded award.

Final Thoughts – Is Erasmus Mundus Right for You?

The Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters Scholarship is a transformative opportunity for international students seeking a world-class education and unique cross-cultural experience in Europe. With comprehensive funding that covers full tuition, a generous monthly stipend (€1,400/month), travel allowance, and health insurance, financial barriers are virtually eliminated .

Beyond the financial benefits, Erasmus Mundus offers an unparalleled academic journey – studying in 2-4 different European countries, earning a prestigious joint degree from multiple universities, and joining a global alumni network of scholars and professionals.

Key deadlines for 2026 intake are approaching or have passed for many programmes. However, some programmes may still accept applications, and the next cycle (for 2027-2029) will open later this year. Start preparing now – research programmes, gather your documents, and craft a compelling application.
