Why Are Delivery Jobs in High Demand in the USA?

The United States faces a significant shortage of delivery drivers due to several converging factors :

  • E-commerce Explosion: Amazon, Walmart, and other retailers have expanded delivery networks exponentially
  • Driver Shortage: The American Trucking Associations reports a historic shortage of commercial drivers
  • Retirement Wave: An aging workforce is leaving the transportation sector
  • High Turnover: The delivery industry experiences regular turnover, creating ongoing job openings
  • Regulatory Changes: New CDL testing rules and English proficiency enforcement are reshaping the workforce landscape

No Degree Required – What You Actually Need

Most delivery driver positions in the United States do not require a college degree or university diploma. Multiple job postings explicitly state "No education required" or "High School Diploma/GED or Equivalent" .

Basic Requirements for Delivery Driver Jobs

  • Valid Driver's License: A valid driver's license is mandatory. Most delivery jobs require a standard Class D license (non-CDL)
  • Age Requirement: Must be at least 21 years old (federal requirement for commercial driving)
  • Clean Driving Record: A clean driving record with no major violations for the past 3 years is essential
  • English Proficiency: Basic English language skills for communicating with customers and reading road signs
  • Physical Fitness: Ability to lift packages weighing 50-70 lbs repeatedly during shifts
  • Drug Testing: Must pass DOT drug test and physical examination
  • Background Check: Pre-employment background check and driving record check required
⚠️ IMPORTANT – CDL Requirements:

Most last-mile delivery positions (Amazon, UPS, FedEx Ground) do NOT require a Commercial Driver's License (CDL). However, positions with food distributors like PepsiCo and US Foods require a valid CDL Class A license . For non-CDL positions, you must be able to obtain a valid non-commercial US driver's license within 15 days of starting employment .

Visa Sponsorship Pathways for Delivery Drivers

1. H-2B Visa (Temporary Non-Agricultural Workers)

The H-2B visa program is the most common pathway for delivery driver positions. This visa allows US employers to hire foreign workers for temporary or seasonal positions .

Key features of H-2B visa:

  • Valid for up to one year (renewable for up to three years)
  • Employer must demonstrate temporary need
  • Cap of 66,000 visas per fiscal year (33,000 for first half, 33,000 for second half)
  • No degree required for delivery positions
  • Prevailing wage must be paid

2. EB-3 Visa (Permanent Residence)

The EB-3 "Other Workers" category is designed for positions requiring less than two years of training or experience – which includes delivery drivers .

Key features of EB-3 visa:

  • Provides a Green Card (permanent residence)
  • Requires Labor Certification (PERM) process
  • Employer must prove no qualified US workers are available
  • Processing typically takes 1-2 years
  • Workers with Green Cards are protected from CDL eligibility restrictions under proposed legislation

3. H-1B Visa (Specialty Occupations)

While less common for delivery drivers, some logistics coordination and dispatcher roles may qualify for H-1B visas .

📌 Important Update: Proposed legislation like "Dalilah's Law" could affect foreign dispatch services and CDL eligibility. Companies are increasingly exploring the EB-3 program to bring key personnel into permanent, compliant roles in the United States . Drivers with Green Cards face no eligibility issues under any version of proposed legislation.

How to Apply for Delivery Driver Jobs with Visa Sponsorship

Step 1: Search for H-2B or EB-3 Sponsored Positions

Use job boards and the official Department of Labor's H-2B job registry to find positions that explicitly offer visa sponsorship. Look for postings that mention "H-2B visa," "EB-3 sponsorship," or "foreign workers welcome."

Step 2: Prepare Your Application

Tailor your resume to highlight your driving experience, clean driving record, physical fitness, and any customer service skills. Mention your willingness to relocate to the United States and your availability for seasonal or permanent work.

📋 Sample Delivery Driver Resume Tips:
  • List years of driving experience and types of vehicles operated
  • Include any delivery or logistics experience (even if informal)
  • Highlight physical fitness and ability to lift 50-70 lbs
  • Mention clean driving record and any safety certifications
  • Include English proficiency level

Step 3: Submit Your Application

Apply directly through employer websites, Amazon DSP job portals, or the Department of Labor's H-2B job registry. The process is free – legitimate employers will never ask you to pay for visa sponsorship.

Step 4: Attend Interviews

If shortlisted, you may be invited to a virtual or in-person interview. Be prepared to discuss your driving experience, physical capabilities, and availability.

Step 5: Receive Job Offer and Visa Sponsorship

Once you receive a job offer, your employer will file the necessary visa petition (H-2B or EB-3) with USCIS. For H-2B positions, the employer must file Form I-129 and obtain temporary labor certification from the Department of Labor.

Step 6: Apply for Your Work Visa

After USCIS approves the petition, you apply for your visa at a US embassy or consulate in your home country. Processing times vary by country.

Can Delivery Drivers Transition to Permanent Residence?

Yes. After gaining experience as a delivery driver, you may be eligible to apply for permanent residence through several pathways :

  • EB-3 "Other Workers" Category: Direct pathway to Green Card for delivery drivers
  • Adjustment of Status: For workers already in the US on H-2B visas
  • Employer Sponsorship: Many companies convert H-2B workers to EB-3 permanent sponsorship

Drivers with Green Cards are significantly advantaged as they are not affected by proposed CDL eligibility restrictions .
