Australia Awards Scholarship 2026 – Fully Funded to Study in Australia

Advertisement
Published: May 18, 2026 Category: Study in Australia

The Australia Awards Scholarship 2026 is a prestigious, fully funded scholarship program administered by the Australian Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) . The scholarship is designed to contribute to the development priorities of Australia's partner countries, with a strong focus on the Indo-Pacific region . For the 2026 intake, applications are currently open until 30 April 2026 for study commencing in 2027 .

Since 1953, Australia Awards Scholarships have played a key role in strengthening relationships between Australia and partner countries, supporting over 100,000 scholars worldwide . The program provides opportunities for students from eligible developing countries to undertake full-time undergraduate or postgraduate study at leading Australian universities and Technical and Further Education (TAFE) institutions .

🚀 Key Takeaway: The Australia Awards Scholarship is a fully funded Australian government scholarship for students from eligible developing countries. Benefits include full tuition fees, a fortnightly living stipend (Contribution to Living Expenses), return airfare, Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC), establishment allowance, and pre-course English training if required . Applications close 30 April 2026 for study commencing in 2027 .

What Does the Australia Awards Scholarship Cover?

The Australia Awards Scholarship is a comprehensive fully funded program that covers virtually all expenses associated with studying in Australia . The scholarship includes:

BenefitDetails
Full Tuition Fees Complete coverage of tuition fees for the approved programme at participating Australian institutions
Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE) Fortnightly stipend paid at a rate set by DFAT to cover accommodation and daily expenses
Return Air Travel One economy-class return flight from your home country to Australia via the most direct route
Establishment Allowance One-off payment to assist with accommodation, textbooks, and study materials upon arrival
Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) Health insurance for the duration of the award (excluding pre-existing conditions)
Introductory Academic Program (IAP) Compulsory pre-study orientation covering living and studying in Australia
Pre-course English (PCE) Training English language training if required, either in-country or in Australia
Supplementary Academic Support Additional academic support where necessary to ensure success
Fieldwork Support For eligible research students or coursework programs with compulsory research components
📌 Important Note: The scholarship covers the minimum period required to complete the approved academic program, including any preparatory training . Students must maintain satisfactory academic progress to continue receiving benefits.

Eligible Countries for Australia Awards Scholarship

Australia Awards Scholarships are available to applicants from a wide range of participating countries, primarily across the Indo-Pacific region, as well as Africa . Eligible countries include:

  • Asia: Bangladesh, Indonesia, Nepal, Malaysia, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, India, Philippines, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Thailand, Timor-Leste
  • Africa: Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, Ghana, Tanzania, Uganda, Botswana, Mozambique, Malawi, Zambia
  • Pacific: Fiji, Papua New Guinea, Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, Samoa, Tonga, Kiribati, Nauru, Tuvalu
  • Other Regions: Mongolia, Maldives, Bhutan, and more
📌 Country-Specific Requirements: Eligibility, priority fields of study, and application processes vary by country . Applicants must review the specific requirements for their country on the official Australia Awards website before applying.

Eligibility Requirements

Eligibility criteria vary slightly by country, but the following general requirements apply to most applicants :

Basic Eligibility

  • Citizenship: Must be a citizen of an eligible country and reside in that country at the time of application
  • Age Requirement: Some countries require applicants to be at least 25 years old by specific dates (varies by country)
  • Work Experience: Many programs require at least 5 years of relevant work experience (especially for African applicants)
  • Academic Qualification: Must hold an undergraduate degree equivalent to an Australian bachelor's degree
  • Professional Employment: Must be currently employed in a field relevant to their intended study
  • English Proficiency: Must meet minimum English language requirements (IELTS 6.5 overall, no band less than 6.0 typically)

Ineligible Applicants

The following individuals are not eligible for Australia Awards Scholarships :

  • Citizens of Australia or New Zealand
  • Individuals who already hold or are undertaking a master's degree
  • Applicants with a criminal record in any country
  • Military personnel (except for those who have been formally discharged)
  • Individuals who have previously received a long-term Australia Award Scholarship
⚠️ CRITICAL – Two-Year Return Requirement:

Recipients of Australia Awards Scholarships are required to return to their home country for a minimum of two years after completing their studies . This is a legally binding condition included in the scholarship contract. Failure to comply may result in the scholar being required to repay the full cost of the scholarship .

Eligible Fields of Study

Australia Awards Scholarships prioritize fields that support sustainable development and regional cooperation . Priority areas vary by country but generally include :

  • Public Policy and Governance: Public administration, policy development, governance reform
  • Health and Medicine: Public health, healthcare management, nursing, medicine
  • Education and Teacher Training: Educational leadership, curriculum development, pedagogy
  • Agriculture and Environmental Management: Sustainable agriculture, natural resource management, climate resilience
  • Engineering, Science, and Technology: Civil, electrical, software engineering; data science; renewable energy
  • Business and Management: Economics, finance, international business, project management
  • Law and Human Rights: International law, human rights law, legal reform
📌 Participating Universities: Australia Awards Scholars can study at leading Australian universities including the University of Sydney, University of Melbourne, Australian National University, University of Queensland, University of Adelaide, University of Western Australia, Monash University, University of New South Wales, and many more .

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Check Your Country's Eligibility

Visit the official Australia Awards website and navigate to your country's specific page. Review the eligibility criteria, priority fields, and application opening/closing dates for your country .

Step 2: Research Participating Universities and Courses

Identify eligible master's or PhD programmes at participating Australian universities. Contact potential supervisors if applying for research programmes . You may need a written in-principle agreement from a supervisor for research degrees .

Step 3: Prepare Required Documents

Gather the following documents before starting your application :

  • Valid Passport – As proof of citizenship
  • Academic Transcripts and Certificates – Certified copies of your bachelor's degree and any postgraduate qualifications
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) – Detailed CV highlighting professional experience
  • Employment Certificate – Proof of current employment
  • Work Experience Proof – Letters from employers documenting relevant experience
  • Two Referee Reports – Academic or professional references
  • Research Proposal – For students enrolling in research programmes
  • Supervisor Agreement – Written in-principle agreement from potential supervisor (for research degrees)
  • English Proficiency Test Results – IELTS (6.5 overall, no band below 6.0), TOEFL (85+), or equivalent
  • Statement of Purpose / Development Proposal – Explaining your study plans and development impact
📝 Pro Tip – Development Impact Statement:

The best-performing applications connect a specific, named research or professional goal directly to a named problem in your country's public sector . Vague development impact statements like "contributing to national growth" rarely survive the shortlisting stage . Be specific about the problem you want to solve and how your Australian education will help you solve it.

Step 4: Submit Your Application via OASIS

All applications must be submitted online through the Online Australia Scholarships Information System (OASIS) . The exceptions are students from the Philippines and Indonesia, who may have different application procedures .

To apply:

  1. Create an account on the OASIS platform
  2. Complete the online application form with your personal, academic, and professional details
  3. Upload all required supporting documents
  4. Review your application carefully before submission
  5. Submit your application before the deadline
📌 Important Application Notes:
  • You can start an application and make changes until the deadline; the application cannot be changed after it has been submitted
  • If you cannot use the online application platform, you may apply via mail
  • Supporting documentation requirements vary by country
  • We strongly encourage applicants to submit their applications well before the deadline, as the online system tends to experience high traffic close to the closing date

Step 5: Await Shortlisting and Interview

After submission, applications undergo an initial screening by the nominating agency . Shortlisted candidates are invited for a panel interview (typically July-August) . Candidates are assessed on:

  • Academic competence and professional achievements
  • Leadership potential and soft skills
  • Potential contribution to development outcomes in their home country
  • Quality of the study/research proposal

Step 6: Receive Final Notification

Successful candidates are notified in late 2026 . Awardees receive an official offer confirming scholarship coverage and must sign a contract with the Commonwealth of Australia agreeing to the award conditions .

Step 7: Apply for Student Visa and Prepare for Departure

After accepting the scholarship, successful applicants must apply for an Australian student visa (Subclass 500) through the Department of Home Affairs . Scholars will also participate in a pre-departure briefing and the Introductory Academic Program (IAP) upon arrival in Australia .

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

When is the deadline for the Australia Awards Scholarship 2026?

The application deadline is 30 April 2026 for study commencing in 2027 .

Can I apply directly to the Australia Awards Scholarship?

Applications must be submitted through the official OASIS online portal, except for students from the Philippines and Indonesia who may have different procedures .

What is the age limit for Australia Awards?

Age requirements vary by country. For example, Kenyan applicants must be at least 25 years old by 1 February 2027 . Check your country-specific page for detailed requirements.

Do I need IELTS for the Australia Awards Scholarship?

Yes, most programs require proof of English proficiency. The typical requirement is IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, TOEFL 85+, or equivalent .

Can I bring my family with me?

The scholarship provides Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the scholar only . Scholars who wish to bring dependents must make their own arrangements and cover additional costs.

What happens if I don't return to my home country after the scholarship?

The scholarship contract requires you to return to your home country for a minimum of two years . Failure to comply may result in DFAT requiring you to repay the full cost of the scholarship as a debt . This is a legally binding condition.

Is the Australia Awards Scholarship competitive?

Yes, Australia Awards Scholarships are highly competitive, with thousands of applications received annually for a limited number of awards . Strong academic records, leadership experience, and compelling development proposals are essential for success .

Final Thoughts – Is Australia Awards Right for You?

The Australia Awards Scholarship is one of the world's most prestigious and comprehensive international scholarship programs, offering fully funded opportunities to study at leading Australian universities for students from eligible developing countries . With full tuition coverage, a generous fortnightly living stipend, return airfare, health insurance, and additional allowances, Australia Awards removes financial barriers to Australian education .

However, Australia Awards is about more than funding – it is a leadership development program that invests in future change-makers who will return to their home countries and contribute to sustainable development . The two-year return requirement is a fundamental condition of the scholarship and cannot be waived .

If you are from an eligible country and you have strong academic credentials, relevant work experience, leadership potential, and a clear vision for how an Australian degree will help you drive development in your home country, the Australia Awards Scholarship could be your pathway to an extraordinary academic and professional journey.
